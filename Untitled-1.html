Extending the End Points:  IQ is a great tool for tracking work within your office.  There are also a number of ways to extend that reach to your users, constituents and others who provide information pertinent to the work that you do.  How?  Consider how data comes in: 

Is this your current office process?

Do you know that IQ allows you to…?

Do you use web forms to collect requests or other information?

 

With web services you can automatically create or update data in your IQ records by linking your web forms directly to your IQ workflows.

 

Do you receive e-mail requests that need to be tracked and managed? 

IQ Internet Mail Agent (IMA) provides fast and efficient processing of all incoming e-mail.  IQ IMA can process formatted or unformatted messages, analyze the content based on your business rules and automatically create and queue new records from these messages.

 

Are you corresponding with other agencies that use IQ or with stakeholders outside of IQ? 

Using IQ Extended Workflow allows both IQ-to-IQ data population and automated syncing of e-mail correspondence back to the originating workflow record. 

 

Have you considered using the IQ Outlook Add-in? 

IQ Outlook Add-in is a great solution for users who spend most of their day in e-mail, allowing them to interact with assignments and other information in IQ directly from Microsoft Outlook.

 

Make it Your Own:  Sometimes, something as simple as modifying or creating custom search screens, reports, dashboards and record layout can transform the IQ experience from good to great.  A minimal investment can result in clearer, more intuitively presented data for your users and better high level understanding for your managers, compliance officers and others involved in tracking how records are processed.

Training:  Advanced training, skills gap analysis, customized e-Learning modules and targeted training on adjacent features are all great ways to ensure your users are fully supported in their use of IQ.  From the administrative side, training is available to allow you to better manage and customize IQ on your own, keeping your total cost of ownership as low as possible.   

Additional Applications:  Typically, IQ is adopted to address a specific set of workflow requirements within an organization.  The most critical needs come first.  But why stop there?  Our customers use IQ for tracking everything from Congressional requests to document management, processing FOIA requests and appeals to taxicab licensing and pothole repairs.  With a platform that is that adaptable, ask yourself and your users how else IQ could be put to use to offer structure and traceability to additional processes within your office. 

Who Else?  The question sounds simple, and it is.  Sometimes the easiest way to extend the benefits you’re already getting from IQ is to bring IQ to more users, allowing them to benefit in the same way your early adopters have.
